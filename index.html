OCEAN (Web-based Data Collection Tool)

This aims to solve a medical data collection problem based on a clinical activity research named “BEACH” (Bettering the Evaluation and Care of Health). This research has been a continuous national study of general practice activity conducted by the Family Medicine Research Centre (FMRC) of the University of Sydney since April 1998.

OCEAN (Web-based Data Collection Tool)

OCEAN embraces the principal processes carried out in the BEACH annual medical survey. This has been designed and implemented as a web-based general activity survey which collects all the information of GP-patient encounters. This information corresponds basically to, GP characteristics, encounter data, patient health status, and primary care details.

This program also implements medical coding system as ICPC-2 which is the national standard in Australia for recording general practice and primary care, and CAPS (Coding Atlas of Pharmaceutical Substances) for drugs and medications.

The resources used for implementing this solution are mainly open-source software. These correspond to AngularJS, Bootstrap, and INSPINIA for the front-end interfaces, and Node.js and MongoDB for the back-end database.

This software is currently installed and running at The University of Sydney for the Faculty of Public Health. OCEAN is now being tested by analysts, coders and GPs.

Cachilupi (didactic kid toys)

This project is the semester assignment of “Multimedia Design and Authoring” UoS in semester 2 - 2015.

This program is inspired by a real kid toys e-commerce website (http://www.cachilupi.cl).

It is based on several multimedia design techniques (e.i. deformation, image texturing, 3D texting, blue screen video production) and tools such as Flash CS6 for animation and authoring, image enhancement with Photoshop CS6, 3D animation (Blender 2.75a), and ActionScript to control UI elements.

3D Mosaic Viewer (3DMV - scripted module)

This program was developed as semester assignment for “Information Technology in Biomedicine” UoS in semester 2 - 2015.

This module creates a 3D Mosaic Viewer for Volumes (NRRD files) and Models (VTK files). This scripted module is built in Python 3.4.3 and VTK (Visualization Toolkit) for 3D Slicer 4.4.
